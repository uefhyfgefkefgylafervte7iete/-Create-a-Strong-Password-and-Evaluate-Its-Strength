## üß† Best Practices for Creating Strong Passwords

1.  Use **at least 12--16 characters** combining upper/lowercase
    letters, numbers, and symbols.
2.  Avoid **common words, personal details, or predictable patterns**
    (e.g., "123", "password", birthdays).
3.  Replace letters with symbols or numbers in a **non-obvious way**
    (e.g., "A" ‚Üí "@", "E" ‚Üí "3").
4.  Use **unique passwords** for every account.
5.  Consider **passphrases** --- a sequence of unrelated words that are
    easier to remember but hard to crack.
6.  Use a **password manager** to securely store and generate complex
    passwords.
7.  Change passwords **regularly** or when a data breach occurs.
8.  Enable **two-factor authentication (2FA)** wherever possible.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## üßæ Tips Learned from the Evaluation

-   Basic passwords are easy to remember but provide **limited
    security**.\
-   Adding symbols, numbers, and mixed-case letters **significantly
    improves strength**.\
-   Passwords with **randomized character placement** are nearly
    impossible to brute-force.\
-   Ultra-complex passwords reach 100% strength but may require a
    **password manager** for storage.\
-   Using a **balance of memorability and randomness** is ideal for most
    users.
